Snap tests and first click analysis

Snap tests and first click analysis both focus on the initial impressions and reactions a user has towards a website.

A snap test shows a user a webpage for a few seconds and then asks that user to recall what they remember and in what order. That helps to ascertain whether users see essential calls to action, while the recall order helps identify whether the page’s visual hierarchy is correct.

A first click test also seeks to better understand a user's initial reaction to a page. It aims to establish where the user would click next when trying to complete a specific task. This is because a participant who clicks down the right path on the first click will complete their task successfully 87 percent of the time.

Because both a snap test and a first click test can be carried out on a non-interactive interface, they are ideal for testing early in the development cycle. Although these tests can be performed on a functioning website, they can easily be used on a prototype, wireframe, or even graphical mockup.

The purpose of this course is to provide a high-level overview of the various types of research techniques, without diving into the nuances of the implementation tactics.

This overview is meant to provide a starting point for further research. With that said, it’s worth exploring both usability testing and multivariate testing in more detail, as these are the most common tools used in ecommerce design and development.
