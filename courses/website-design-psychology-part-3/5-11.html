A/B and multivariate testing

A/B and multivariate testing is another example of testing suited to a website that has already gone live. This form of testing is recognized as one of the most common mechanisms for growth on ecommerce sites.

A/B and multivariate testing allow merchants to experiment with functionality and calls to action to ascertain which approach has the most positive impact on conversion.

For example, a merchant may be considering multiple versions of a product promotion that offers different levels of discounts. Using A/B testing that merchant could show different versions of the promotion to a small percentage of users visiting the website. The promotion that proves the most successful could then be rolled out to all users thereby maximizing conversion.

A/B testing focuses on making only a single change at any one time, while multivariate testing will monitor the impact of changing multiple elements simultaneously. For example, an A/B test would change the text on a button, while multivariate testing might change the text, the color, and the placement of a button.
