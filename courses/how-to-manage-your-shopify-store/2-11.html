Shipping Settings


Success in ecommerce can largely be impacted by how a product order is fulfilled, and whether shipping is optimized for profitability. Shipping is the final step in the transactional customer experience. The speed of delivery and the overall shipping experience can affect repeat sales, reviews, and word of mouth recommendations.

Shipping strategy

The real challenge when figuring out your shipping strategy is determining a solution that cuts into margins as little as possible and yet remains attractive to customers. Whether you are self shipping, warehousing, or dropshipping, you can set up their preferred shipping methods from the shipping settings page.

Merchants have options in how they can set up shipping rates in the Shopify Admin. They include:

  • Free shipping
  • Carrier calculated rates
  • Flat rates
  • A combination of any of the three.

There are three ways to access the carrier calculated rates feature:

  • Option A: The feature is already included in the Shopify Advanced plan
  • Option B: Merchants can contact Shopify Support to add real-time shipping for $20 a month to the Basic or Shopify plan
  • Option C: Merchants can choose the annual billing period on the Basic or Shopify plan, then Contact Shopify Support to add real-time shipping at no additional cost

Handling backorders and returns

Before you launch your store, you should have a plan for handling backorders and returns. If you choose to track inventory with Shopify and allow overselling on sold out products, you’ll need to consider the customer service implications and create a communication and fulfillment workflow to address those cases.

If you don’t allow overselling when products are sold out, you can keep customers engaged and recover potential lost sales by putting in place some simple customizations on the store’s product page to allow customers to sign up for back in stock notifications. You can also use apps like Back In Stock, or Back in Stock Alerts.

You can set products to be removed from the store automatically using automated collections, or hide sold out products from the store manually or using an app like Wipeout.

Scaling your shipping strategy

As your business grows and order volume increases, the store’s shipping strategy will evolve. This may require the support of warehousing solutions or inventory apps with shipping capabilities to further automate fulfillment workflows.
