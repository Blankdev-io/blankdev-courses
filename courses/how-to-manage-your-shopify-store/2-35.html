Social Media


Shopify empowers merchants to sell on social media and find new customers on platforms where buyers already spend a lot of their time. Stores can connect to Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, and Pinterest channels.

Facebook

Shopify’s Facebook sales channel allows customers to browse products and checkout from a merchant’s Facebook business page. Once connected, Facebook will create a shop tab on the business page, where synced Shopify collections and products will appear.

Instagram

Merchants who set up the Facebook sales channel can also enable the Instagram sales channel. The Instagram sales channel allows merchants to tag products in their posts. Customers can then purchase those products from a webview within the Instagram app.

Facebook Messenger channel

Innovations in conversational commerce are saving busy shop owners time while providing a more personalized experience for customers. Bots like the Messenger channel function are driving sales to stores through conversation.

When the Facebook Messenger sales channel is added to a Shopify store’s admin, customers can opt in at checkout to receive Messenger order updates. They'll receive an order confirmation, a notification when their order is out for delivery, and shipping updates. They'll also have the ability to shop for other products directly from the conversations in Messenger.

Selling on Pinterest

Pinterest has proven to be a significant traffic referrer for Shopify stores whose audience match the platform’s core users. The Shopify Masters podcast episode This Home Décor Store Drives 93% of Its Social Traffic From Pinterest is a case study on pinning strategies that drive qualified traffic to ecommerce businesses.

As a part of their Pinterest strategy, merchants can turn Pins with online store product links into Buyable Pins so customers can shop directly from Pinterest.
