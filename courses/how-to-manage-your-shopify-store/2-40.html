Awareness

At this stage in the adoption process, the focus is brand visibility. The 50 Ways to Make Your First Sale guide has actionable tactics for promotion through earned, owned, and paid media. It encourages merchants to make steps towards the goal of building a strong web presence, before investing cash into advertising, with steps for often overlooked free marketing activities like public relations, in-person marketing, and digital publishing.

SEO

Merchants can set up Google My Business for their online store, to show up in map results beside other local businesses as a part of their search engine optimization efforts.

As we covered in previous chapters, there are built-in areas in the Shopify Admin where merchants can ensure content is written with SEO in mind. This is the first step to encouraging discoverability of products, collections, blog posts, and the homepage.

We also recommend creating an account with Google Search Console to submit a sitemap to Google, and ask Google to index webpages. This will help the store appear in results when shoppers are searching for similar products.

Social media

Social media is relevant in all stages of the buyer’s adoption process, with different content strategies for each stage. There are a lot of great articles about driving sales referred from social networks in the social media marketing category on the Shopify Blog. Read articles that cover how to make the best of Facebook marketing, growing a following on Instagram, and using content on platforms like YouTube to drive sales:

  • Facebook
  • Instagram
  • YouTube

Merchants can also share products and collections on Facebook directly from the admin.

Track clicks from social media activity when you use a UTM builder. Traffic from these links show up in the visitors by marketing campaign reports in Shopify and in Google Analytics.

Advertising

Adwords is built so brands can create and publish ads for each stage of a buyer’s journey, store owners can track Google AdWords conversions in the online store.

There are also apps that can be used to help merchants with their marketing. For example, Kit is a virtual employee that automates paid advertising on Facebook and saves entrepreneurs time. Merchants can use many different Shopify apps to automate other marketing activities.

Tracking is an important, and often forgotten, part of marketing. It’s key to understanding how advertising efforts like guest posts, cross promotions, and influencer marketing impact a business.

A UTM builder or trackable link like bit.ly can ensure you measure what traffic and sales are attributed to which campaign and source. For example, if you share an embed code for a buy button with a blogger or influencer, which creates content for the store’s target audience, you can track how many conversions happen via their content.
