Apps


Apps are an extension of the Shopify core product. Other platforms call these plugins, modules, or add-ons. Apps are created by Shopify and app development partners to extend the core functionality of the platform and meet specific merchant needs in the following categories:

  • Product sourcing
  • Sales and marketing
  • Shipping and inventory
  • Social media
  • Customer service
  • Accounting and reporting

Other criteria for justifying adding an app to the store are the ROI for paid apps, whether the app will save you time, and how it will make management of the store more efficient.

Key considerations

  • Cost (free/paid/recurring cost/billing cycles)
  • Desired functionality (whether it's available in the core product, in the app store, or needs to be custom build)
  • Purpose
  • Reliability (ratings, compatibility, experience with the app or developer, uncertainty/unknowns, free trial period)

Types of apps

There are three main categories of apps. They are published apps, custom apps, and private apps:

  • Published apps: These apps, available from the Shopify App Store serve multiple shops and users. They are rigorously reviewed by Shopify to ensure they meet certain service level expectations.
  • Custom apps: You can create custom apps. These apps can give you the ability to add features to the Shopify Admin, access the store's data directly using Shopify's APIs, or extend the online store to other platforms using custom storefronts.
  • Private apps: Created on the store level, these apps empower you to create backend operations or custom storefronts using Shopifyâ€™s APIs. Unlike custom apps, these cannot be used to create interfaces or add features to the Shopify admin.

Developing Apps

Some common examples of app building that might be required when building a store are:

  • Building an importer to migrate your data from Magento or another provider to Shopify
  • Integrating your store's data with a custom solution that you're using to manage your orders, products, or inventory outside of Shopify
  • Offering products from your Shopify store on a custom storefront, like a mobile app, website, or video game
