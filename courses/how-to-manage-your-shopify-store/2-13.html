Tax Settings


Depending on your location, you may need to register your business with local or federal tax authorities to handle taxes for your store. It’s important to know that Shopify doesn’t file or remit your sales taxes on your behalf. However, Shopify provides calculations and reports to help merchants stay organized when it’s time to file and remit taxes. Merchants should seek specific tax advice from a local tax authority or professional accountants.

Shopify Plus merchants have access to the Avalara AvaTax suite that automates tax calculations and the tax filing process. Avalara provides real-time tax codes from more than 12,000 taxing jurisdictions, which helps make sure that your sales tax is calculated based on the most up-to-date tax rules.

In the admin, taxes are calculated based on the customer’s address for each order. So, it’s important to set up shipping settings first as they affect the options that are available in your tax settings page. This tutorial guides you through the general process to set up taxes.

The admin has some specific considerations and settings for stores based in the following regions:

  • USA: Understanding nexus and automatic tax rates available
  • India: How to set up GST in India
  • European Union: How to show VAT prices on your product pages and digital product taxes

Advanced tax settings

The Shopify Admin makes accommodations for scenarios where a merchant needs to include taxes in product prices, override default tax settings, and charge taxes on shipping rates.

You can enable these for your store in one step by checking the following features on the taxes page:

  • Include taxes in pricing
  • Charge taxes on shipping
