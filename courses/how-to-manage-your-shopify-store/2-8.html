Checkout Settings


The checkout is where most people abandon their purchase. Shopify provides configuration and styling options so you can offer the best checkout experience for customers, reducing frustrations that affect cart abandonment. Extensive editing to the checkout is also available for Shopify Plus merchants.

Customizing the checkout style

The theme editor allows you to customize the style of your checkout pages to match your store’s branding. Style elements you can edit include:

  • Logo
  • Accent colors
  • Brand or theme fonts
  • Background color or image

Customer accounts

Making customer accounts mandatory can contribute to a higher abandoned cart rate. Therefore, merchants can make customer accounts optional or disable them completely.

Customers can use their accounts to track current order status, view order history, and speed up the checkout process.

Customer Contact

As more shoppers make mobile the primary devices they complete transactions on, offering the option to enter a cell phone number instead of an email at the first stage of checkout can improve the overall post-transaction experience. Notifications can then be sent by SMS rather than email.

However, note that your email marketing efforts could be significantly affected if the number of shoppers checking out using a phone number increases, as they will lose the ability to collect emails from existing customers.

To provide customer service by SMS while keeping email address as the main customer contact method, you can add an opt-in for SMS order updates.

Checkout form options

You can configure the appearance of the following checking form options:

  • Full name
  • Company name
  • Address line 2
  • Shipping address
  • Phone number

When setting up form options, consider these tips based on common scenarios:

  • Make address line 2 optional instead of mandatory if the merchant ships to residential addresses
  • Make phone number mandatory so you can contact the customer if there are any questions or concerns regarding their order

Order processing

This section controls how a store responds to an order being paid. When an order is marked as fulfilled, a shipping notification gets sent to the customer. The system can either automatically mark an order’s line items as fulfilled, or require a staff member to manually click ‘fulfill’ in the order summary page. Automatic fulfillment is the most hands-off way to fulfill orders, but it is only suitable for some types of products. For stores that hold their own inventory or have a processing time before shipping out their products, manual fulfillment may be the best option.

Additional scripts

You can use the checkout scripts field to add conversion tracking to your order status page or add other Javascript that affects the checkout.

Abandoned checkout options

The checkout is where most people abandon their purchase. Shopify provides configuration so you can send follow up emails to customers who have abandoned their carts, in order to complete the sale.

Checkout language

You can translate the checkout language within the store’s language editor from the checkout settings
