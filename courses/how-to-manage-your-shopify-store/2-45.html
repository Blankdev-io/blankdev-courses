Analytics


Shopify's analytics and reports allows merchants to view a store's recent activity, get insight into visitors, and analyze the store's transactions. For advanced reporting within Shopify, your client can upgrade their plan from their account settings.

Overview dashboard, live view, and finance reports are accessible on all plans with an online store sales channel. The following reports vary by plan:

  • Acquisition
  • Behavior
  • Marketing
  • Sales
  • Customers
  • Custom

Merchants can filter and export various reports for accounting purposes. Those who use external accounting systems can export sales data with apps like Intuit Quickbooks Online.

We encourage merchants to pair Shopify Analytics with Google Analytics for reporting. Enabling Google Analytics Enhanced Ecommerce and configuring goals and funnels will allow for better tracking.

Tax finance report

The taxes report, available on all Shopify plans, shows the sales taxes that were applied to your sales in the selected timeframe.

1099-K Forms for US Merchants

In the US, Shopify is required to submit a 1099-K form to the IRS for each merchant who uses Shopify Payments, processes more than $20,000, and has more than 200 transactions in a calendar year. This form is generated by Shopify and provided to both the merchant and the IRS on an annual basis for every shop that qualifies.

Access to this form can be found on the payouts page once made available each year.
