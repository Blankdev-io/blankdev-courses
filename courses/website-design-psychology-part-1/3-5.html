Perception of ease


The last element to consider when reducing cognitive load is aesthetics. Aesthetics need to be created in such a way that they will not reduce the mental effort involved in using an interface, but it will alter our perception of that load.

We have an inherent bias towards things we perceive as attractive. If something looks visually pleasing, we tend to see it in a more positive light, including how easy it is to use. It feels more natural to use, and so we tend to persevere longer.

Aesthetics have other psychological benefits too. Attractiveness is linked with trustworthiness and value as well, because of something called the halo effect. Our templates are carefully crafted to not only be attractive from a psychological aspect, but they are also optimized to increase sales and customer retention, you can check them here: themes.blankdev.io

The halo effect is our tendency to judge an entire experience based on a single, initial characteristic. So in the case of an ecommerce site, a user’s initial impression of the design could be enough to undermine the sense of trust in your offering. In other words, unrelated factors can influence how we feel about a company and, by extension, our likelihood to complete a call to action.
