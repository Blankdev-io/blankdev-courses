Understanding the customer journey


Users do not go from landing on an ecommerce store to placing an order in a single step. It is a journey involving discovering a need, researching options, placing an order, receiving a delivery, and even after-sale support. This journey often happens over an extended period and can involve many visits to an ecommerce site.

The result of this extensive buying cycle is that it may be necessary to present users with different calls to action at different points in the journey. For example, while researching a purchase, we may encourage users to sign up for a newsletter so that they do not forget the store when it comes time to place an order. Equally, after a user has placed an order, we will want to make it clear how to look up their order status to reduce support costs.

Consider that all orders are not equally valuable to those running ecommerce sites. A Shopify merchant may want to push users towards a particular product that offers a higher margin or up-sell additional items to increase the average order value.

All of this means that a well-designed ecommerce site has to do a lot more than just offering an obvious add to cart button. Instead, each touchpoint needs to be carefully thought out to ensure that the right call to action is presented at the right time in the userâ€™s journey.

Understanding where the user is on their purchasing journey is crucial to success when using calls to action. For example, asking them to sign up for a newsletter as they are about to make a purchase is nothing but a distraction. However, asking them to do so on their first visit, before they are ready to buy, makes sense because it helps cement your brand in their minds.
